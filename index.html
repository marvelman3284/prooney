    Overview:

    One of the most important aspects of fitness is training. Training has countless benefits,
    such as, weight management, strengthened bones and muscles, increased lifespan, among
    many other things. In this section I will focus on two main aspects of training:
    strength and cariovascular conditioning. I will also spend time discussing a crosssection
    of these two aspects: CrossFit training.
    First, let's discuss strength training. For a beginner or someone with a busy schedule,
    three thirty to forty-five minute sessions per week is sufficient. For those who are more
    advanced or have more time, you can train more often, but I would not exceed five or six strength
    sessions per week. I will address this more in depth in the "recovery" section, but for now
    let's stick to a three time per week plan. For this type of plan, I recommend doing one upper
    body workout, one lower body workout, and one full body workout. For all of these workouts,
    we will focus on compound movements. Compound movements workout multiple muscle groups
    against the most resistance, therefore they are the most efficient exercises. For upper body,
    pick one compound movement that targets the chest muscles, one that targets the back muscles,
    and one that that targets the shoulders.
